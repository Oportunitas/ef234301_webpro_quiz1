Touring Palembang

-Palembang offers a range of interesting tourist attractions that reflect its cultural and historical significance-

Ampera Bridge

This iconic bridge is a symbol of Palembang and spans the Musi River. It's especially picturesque when lit up at night.

Palembang Light Rail Transit

Explore Palembang's modern side by taking a ride on the Palembang Light Rail Transit (LRT) system. Not only is it a convenient mode of transportation, but it also offers tourists a unique perspective of the city.

Kemaro Island

Located in the Musi River, Kemaro Island is known for its Buddhist temple, Vihara Ksitigarbha Bodhisattva, and the legend of the "Green Stone" related to Chinese-Malay folklore.

Musi River

Enjoy a boat ride along the Musi River to explore the city's waterfront, see traditional houses on stilts, and take in scenic views of Palembang.

Punti Kayu Park

A family-friendly park with a zoo, mini-golf, and recreational facilities. It's a great place to relax and enjoy outdoor activities.

Sultan Mahmud Badaruddin II Museum

This museum offers insights into Palembang's history and culture, showcasing artifacts, traditional textiles, and historical displays.

Kuto Besak Fortress

An old Dutch colonial fortress that has been restored and now serves as a historical landmark.

Bayt Al-Qur'an Al-Akbar

This mosque is one of the largest in Indonesia and is known for its impressive Islamic architecture. Visitors are welcome to explore the mosque's grounds.
